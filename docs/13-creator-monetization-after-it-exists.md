# Creator Monetization — Plan for After It Exists

Date: 2026-05-26

A **post-validation** go-to-market plan. It does **not** change the probe (`docs/12`) and is
inert until both triggers below are met. Captured now because it resolves the A-vs-B fork
(`docs/11` §1.1) in favor of **B — creators as the primary buyer** — but deliberately parked
so it can't pull scope forward.

## Triggers (do not start any of this before both)

1. **The probe cleared** (`docs/12`): extraction beats the baseline on the pre-registered
   thresholds, and at least one real person curated the *actual* pipeline output (B1).
2. **A working single-creator pipeline exists:** a creator can take their own back-catalog →
   review/curate → publish a pack, end to end.

Until both hold, this document is a parking lot, not a plan.

## The bet

Evidence-based / technical creators turn their **own** content into sellable, source-anchored
systems/models packs. The creator is the user *and* the seller. Why this is the right
monetization frame — it inverts the three risks that held the product score down:

- **Copyright → gone.** The creator owns the source, so redistribution is fine. The
  "minimal-excerpt / legally untested" problem (`docs/11` §1.4) evaporates.
- **Demand → an existing money flow.** Creators already pay for production tools and already
  sell to audiences (courses, memberships, Gumroad). No new willingness-to-pay to invent.
- **Cold-start → the creator's own audience.** No two-sided marketplace to bootstrap. Each
  creator brings demand. You are the rails, not the bazaar.

## Segment (narrow on purpose)

Fitness, health, finance, science, skill-teaching — niches where "a receipt for every claim"
is a real trust differentiator and even a liability shield. **Not** personality/entertainment
creators, who sell narrative, not structure. "Content creators" broadly is the wrong target;
"evidence-based educators with a paying audience" is the right one.

## The new risk this introduces

One risk replaces three: **does the audience pay a premium for structured + sourced +
verifiable over just buying the course/video?** The pack may be a *complement* (a premium
study companion) rather than the product, and this puts you adjacent to crowded course
platforms (Teachable / Kajabi / Skool / Maven). The differentiator has to be the
verifiability itself — receipts, not vibes — or there's nothing to charge for.

## Product shape (creator-facing)

Ingest own catalog → curate via the trust workflow → publish a pack → sell to own audience.
Position the pack as either a **premium study companion** to an existing course/membership or
a **standalone sourced reference**. The trust workflow (review/anchor/support_state) is the
creator's craft surface, not a chore — it's what makes the artifact worth more than an AI
summary anyone could DIY.

## Monetization (choose after the first sale, not now)

- Flat tool fee — creator pays to produce packs.
- Take-rate — a % of pack sales.
- Hybrid.

Lean: start with whichever rides an *existing* seller's willingness to pay (a creator who
already sells courses), so you're not also creating a new buying behavior.

## Validation gate for this stage (its own falsifiable test)

**One creator sells one pack to ~20 real buyers at a real price, with a non-embarrassing
refund/repeat signal.** Until that single fact exists, there is no marketplace, no platform
build, and no GTM spend.

## Stays out until the gate above is passed

Marketplace, multi-creator discovery/search, MCP / registry / "lingua franca," payments
infrastructure beyond the simplest possible, and any "platform" framing. Those are downstream
of *many* creators independently succeeding — which is downstream of *one*.
